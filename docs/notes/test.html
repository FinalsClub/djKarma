June, 2007

The 46th Annual Philadelphia Folk Festival – Society Sales Committee

(Including CD sales & camping Ice sales volunteers)



Dear Volunteer,

I hope this letter finds you well and ready for another exciting Fest! If you are a first time Volunteer, some of the following may not make sense. If you have been with us in the past, read on. In either case, there is important information here.

If you have any questions please feel free to contact me – by email is best, Tom.McGee@Verizon.Net.

Plans for the 46th Annual Philadelphia Folk Festival have been underway for several months, and now is the time for the Society Sales crew to begin preparations for what we hope will be another great year!

The only thing that remains the same…
Again this year there are several changes that affect us. The most notable is the weekend. Beginning last year, Fest is now being held on the third weekend in August. This year’s dates are Thursday, August 16 through Sunday, August 19. Check your calendar now to make sure you have it marked correctly.

As I am sure you are aware, the festival has been the main fund raising event for the Philadelphia Folk Song Society. However, many of the recent years have not been financial successes. With reduced resources the Society has had to suspend grant programs, reduce educational programs, make venue changes, and programming adjustments. In fact, profitability has declined to the point where the future of the festival may be in jeopardy. The services provided for volunteers (water, port-a pots, food, etc.) are a significant expense. In order to improve profitability, and help insure the future of the festival, the society has decided to institute a volunteer fee. Adult volunteers, those 18 years of age and older will pay $20. Those volunteers who are 12 to 17 years of age will pay $10. A great value considering you will be able to enjoy over 65+ hours of music plus dancing, crafts, children’s activities, meals, snacks, and camping. All Festival with Camping tickets will be sold for $160 in 2007.

Although the tent will not be open for sales on Thursday, we will hold a sidewalk clearance sale. This sale, open to all volunteers, will feature deep discounts on closeout and low inventory items. Volunteers will also receive a 10% discount on purchases made anytime throughout the weekend. We will be open for business from 11:00 AM until after the end of the evening concert on Friday, Saturday and Sunday.

Folksong Society Membership
All volunteers at the festival are required to be members of the Philadelphia Folk Song Society. Current PFS members already, of course, meet this requirement. If you are not a member, a Festival Associate membership is available for $15. Other memberships start at $25 ($30 for students).

Go to our website at www.pfs.org for information on all levels of membership. Pick the one best suited for you, or your family (family includes two adults and all kids up to age 16). Then sign up on line, or use the downloadable PDF form. All people picking up a Volunteer Badge will be required to be a member at some level. “Festival Associate” members will have until Sept. 30, 2007 to apply their $15 to any other Society membership if they would like to upgrade.

Committee Structure and Shifts

I am happy to say that most of our staff will be returning again this year although we do have one big change to announce. Jim Klingler has decided to retire from the Chairman’s post. John McGlinchey will be taking over the committee and Jim will be providing support to John this year. Jim will be in charge of the Thursday night party and is continuing to guide the buying sub-committee. Our committee leadership this year will be John McGlinchey, Chairman; Jim Klingler, Consultant, myself, Tom McGee, Assistant-Chair in charge of Operations; Assistant-Chairs: Cherie Cremer, Merchandise;; Dan Ruvin, ICE; Ryan Conrad, CD’s, Marie Dolton, Exchequer and many of our Shift Leaders and Head Cashiers will be returning too. Bob Beach will be handling pre-fest communications. Responses to this letter will be directed to him.



Which brings us to Festival Shifts? As in past years, it is required that each volunteer work three five-hour shifts – one each day of Fest: Friday, Saturday, and Sunday (except where noted below). Each volunteer on the regular schedule will be asked to pick one 1st shift, one 2nd shift and one 3rd shift. This makes scheduling completely fair to everyone. The following is the tentative breakdown of shift times:

You will be able to choose either ONE shift a day – Friday, Saturday, and Sunday – or ONE of the Option packages.

Store Hours: Friday: 11am to 12:30am | Saturday: 11am to 12:30am | Sunday: 11am to 10pm

Shifts (Fri-Sun): 1st: 10:30am to 3:30pm | 2nd: 3:00 to 8:00pm | 3rd: 7:30pm to 12:30am

Remember that the store will be open for some time after the end of the evening concert. Third shift will end when all closing activities are completed and you are released by your shift leader. Third shift Sunday will work until about 12:30 to assist the inventory crew. If you have to leave early on Sunday, do not pick this shift!

Options:

Pre-Fest Setup Crew - Positions on this crew are limited and are by invitation only.

Post-Fest Teardown Crew - This will be a crew of able bodies who will be working on the Breakdown of all our hardware; i.e. shelves, racks, tables, etc, plus the loading of the inventory trucks. This crew will work Monday, August 21st plus ONE regular Fest shift listed above – this will count as their three days of work. Note, Monday will be hard, heavy work and may last 4-6 hours. I will only be accepting 20 -25 people for this crew.

Inventory Crew - This crew will work Sunday night from 7pm until the inventory is completed (approximately 5-7 hours) plus ONE regular Fest shift listed above – this will count as their three days of work. I will only be accepting 20 -25 people for this crew.



Shifts will be assigned on a first come, first served basis. We cannot guarantee that you will be scheduled for the shifts you request. Please be sure to confirm your schedule at the tent before Friday morning.



Check www.FolkFest.org for performer schedules so that you can select your shifts to not conflict with the performance of your favorite performers as best you can.



Pre-Fest Early Arrivals

Pre-fest admissions will again be limited this year. Camping gates for Volunteers will not open until 5pm Tuesday, August 15th. For those of you arriving at fest to camp before Thursday, August 19th, you will need a Pre-Fest Pass. Please indicate your arrival date on the form. Passes will be distributed during the pre-fest work weekend (let me know if you can not attend). Pre-fest volunteer food will be served on Wednesday only (breakfast at 8:00, lunch at 12, and supper at 5:30).

The deal with Pre-Fest Arrivals is this; you can choose either Tuesday the 15th or Wednesday the 16th as your arrival date. You are required to work from 10:30am to 2:30pm starting the day AFTER you arrive at Fest, then your regular three shifts. i.e. if your arrival date is Tuesday before Fest, you will be required to work Wednesday and Thursday, plus your three shifts during Fest. If you cannot work the days after your arrival date, then do not request that date. If you are issued a Pre-Fest pass and you do not show up to work the days following your arrival, your Volunteer’s Badge will be pulled. If you have any questions about this please let me know. I am very serious about this and so is the Festival Management.



Pre-Fest Set-up Dates: Saturday, August 11th & Sunday, August 11th

We use the weekend before Fest to move in the old and new inventory. We will be working from 10:00am to 3pm on Saturday and 11 to 3 on Sunday. Lunch will be provided around noon on both days. And who said there’s no free lunch? There will be two shifts on Saturday and one Shift on Sunday. It is asked that each volunteer attend at least one of these shifts. The first Shift on Saturday will be a heavy lifting shift so we really need all those buff, tough volunteers to come out to help us unload the trucks. The second Saturday shift will be lighter duty (compared to Truck unloading) and will be sorting out the inventory for storage. Sunday will continue the inventory sorting work.



Orientation will be held on each of these days immediately following lunch. It is important that each volunteer, especially NEW volunteers, go through Orientation. If you cannot make any of these dates, due to work or that you live far, far away, please let me know in advance.



Friends/Family as Volunteers

We are always happy when members of our committee (family) get friends and family to volunteer with us. Remember, the more volunteer help we have, the easier it is on the rest of us.

  • If you have a friend or family member who would like to volunteer with us for the first time, please include their name, address, and phone number on the back of page 4. If they are coming from another committee please include that committee name.

  • Volunteers without a family member at Fest must be 18 or older.

  • If you have a child who is now 13 to 18 who would like to volunteer please include their name of page 4.


Beverage Fund

We are doing the Juice/Water/Food thing again this year. At your Orientation, please bring $2.00 with you, so we can have plenty of cold beverages and snacks. I will be collecting the money.


Wikee Wakee Woo – which translated means: Society Sales Volunteer Party!!!

The party the past years has been such a hit that we plan to have another this year: Thursday, August 16th starting around 6 pm at the tent. The theme of this party is a Hawaiian Luau (again. since we already have the decorations). So unpack those Hawaiian shirts and plan to get wikee wakee with the rest of the woo, I mean crew! All members of the committee (sales, Cd’s, Camping Ice) are encouraged to attend. We will provide some food, but please plan to bring some food, snacks, and/or beverages to share. There will also be prizes and an open mic - you will not want to miss this! We also ask that everyone who attends the party please donate $3 per person ($9 max per family) to cover the food cost. Again, we'd like to lessen the financial burden on the Festival and fend for ourselves. I will collect the money during pre-fest workdays.



www.folkfest.org

The Folk Festival and Folksong Society have a wonderful website (thanks to John Wilson)! If you have not had a chance to check it out, please do so. There is a ton of information about Fest including who will be performing this year, plus information about other Society events.
